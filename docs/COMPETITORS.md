# YeboBank - Competitor Analysis

## The Gap

No existing product combines all of:
1. Open source
2. M-Pesa integration
3. Savings with interest
4. Chama group wallets
5. Agent cash network
6. Lightning addresses
7. Global payment receiving

YeboBank fills this gap.

## Competitor Detail

### Galoy / Blink
URLs: https://galoy.io | https://blink.sv | https://github.com/GaloyMoney/galoy
What: Open-source Bitcoin bank backend. Powers Blink wallet in El Salvador.
Their strength: Best existing open-source banking stack. Battle-tested. Well-funded.
What they missed: M-Pesa, chamas, agent network, interest engine, Kenya context.
Relationship to YeboBank: Study their architecture closely. Do not fork it (different stack).
Contact: https://galoy.io/contact

### Machankura
URL: https://8333.mobi
What: Lightning over USSD for feature phones. No smartphone needed.
Their strength: Works without internet. Biggest African Bitcoin innovation.
What they missed: No savings. No interest. No chamas. No agents. Wallet only.
Relationship to YeboBank: PARTNER with them. They reach feature phone users.
YeboBank reaches smartphone users. Together: full Kenya coverage.
Twitter: @machankura8333

### Strike
URL: https://strike.me | https://strike.me/blog/announcing-strike-africa/
What: Best Lightning infrastructure globally. Active African expansion.
Their strength: $80M+ funded. Excellent UX. Reliable infrastructure.
What they missed: Closed source. Remittances only. Not a bank. No savings.
Relationship to YeboBank: Not a competitor at launch. YeboBank is a bank.
Strike is a payment rail. Could be an integration partner.

### Bitnob
URL: https://bitnob.com
What: African Lightning wallet + remittances. M-Pesa on/off ramp via partners.
Their strength: African-founded. Established. Good product.
What they missed: Closed source. No savings. No chamas. No agents.
Relationship to YeboBank: Direct competitor in the Kenyan market. Differentiate on
open source, savings product, and community features (chamas + agents).

### BTCPay Server
URL: https://btcpayserver.org | https://github.com/btcpayserver/btcpayserver
What: Open-source Bitcoin payment processor. Used by 5,000+ merchants.
Their strength: 8 years of production hardening. Huge community.
What they missed: Merchant-facing only. No user accounts. No savings.
Relationship to YeboBank: Not a competitor. Integrate BTCPay as an option for
merchant accounts within YeboBank.

### LNbits
URL: https://lnbits.com | https://github.com/lnbits/lnbits
What: Extensible Lightning accounting layer.
Their strength: Huge extension ecosystem. Very flexible.
What they missed: Developer tool, not end-user product. No KYC path. No M-Pesa.
Relationship to YeboBank: Not a competitor. Different audience entirely.

### Yellow Card
URL: https://yellowcard.io
What: African crypto on/off ramp. 20 countries. $215M raised.
Their strength: Deep liquidity. Established. Regulated in multiple countries.
What they missed: Exchange only. Not a bank. No savings. No Lightning.
Relationship to YeboBank: Use Yellow Card as a rate reference or liquidity partner.
They are not a competitor — different product category.

### Chipper Cash
URL: https://chippercash.com
What: Mobile money across Africa. 5M+ users.
Their strength: Massive user base. Multi-country.
What they missed: No Bitcoin. No savings interest. No Lightning.
Relationship to YeboBank: YeboBank users can receive from Chipper Cash via M-Pesa.
Not a technical competitor — completely different underlying technology.

### M-Pesa (Safaricom)
What: The dominant mobile money system in Kenya. 51M users.
Their strength: Ubiquitous. Deep carrier integration. Government trusted.
What they missed: Not savings. Inflation erodes M-Pesa balances. No Bitcoin.
Relationship to YeboBank: YeboBank RUNS ON TOP of M-Pesa. It is the on-ramp
and off-ramp, not the competitor.

## Positioning Statement

YeboBank is the only open-source, community-operated Bitcoin bank that:
- Works with M-Pesa (not against it)
- Pays interest on savings (in sats)
- Supports group savings (chamas, SACCOs)
- Enables a community agent cash network
- Receives global payments in any currency
- Requires only a phone number to open

Target user: someone who already saves with a chama, sends money via M-Pesa,
and is watching the KES lose 7% per year to inflation.
They do not need to know what Bitcoin is.
They need to know: "your savings earn here, and the money doesn't shrink."
